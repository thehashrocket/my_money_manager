Ledger Paper

A warm, analog design language for a local-first budgeting tool. Borrowed from paper ledgers, bank tickers, and cheap notebooks — kept quiet, kept personal.

Principle 01

Paper before pixels

Warm off-whites, hand-ruled dividers, a whisper of texture. Nothing glossy, nothing corporate. It should feel like a notebook you actually use.

Principle 02

Numbers are the hero

Geist Mono with tabular figures for every dollar amount. Color used sparingly — banking-parens for negatives, ledger-green restraint for positives.

Principle 03

Quiet, but opinionated

One terracotta accent for primary action. One ledger-green for gain, one red-brown for loss. That’s it. Chroma held at 0.09 across the palette.

Money

03 · display conventions
Negatives wrap in parentheses — banking tradition, no minus ambiguity. Positives drop the plus sign. Zero goes muted. Color is restrained: ledger-green only for gains you want to feel good about (deposits, under-budget); red-brown for overspends and withdrawals in totals, but never for individual transaction rows (most spending is already expected).
